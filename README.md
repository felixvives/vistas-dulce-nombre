# Casa Dulce Nombre — Remodeling Vision

A single-page architectural vision for the open-concept, contemporary remodeling of a
1996 two-story house in **Residencial Las Vistas de Dulce Nombre, Cartago, Costa Rica**.

The document translates raw property data — photos, the cadastral plan, and registry
records — into a concrete design direction for a professional architect.

The strongest asset is the site: the lot fronts the street to the west and backs onto the
**Quebrada Cuzo protection zone** to the east — a legally protected green buffer that
guarantees no construction behind the house and gives the rear garden a permanent screen of
mature tropical vegetation. The whole design is oriented toward that green edge.

---

## The vision

**Objective — modernize without rebuilding.** The constraints are cosmetic and spatial
rather than structural: a sound shell, a recently repainted exterior, generous existing
window openings, and an east-facing garden. The investment can go where it changes daily
life.

**Design direction — warm minimalism.** Clean lines and quiet surfaces, warmed by wood and
the green light of the creek corridor. One continuous, daylit ground-floor volume that opens
to the east.

- **Connect the spaces.** Remove the partitions between kitchen, dining, and living so the
  three read as one open volume — island as anchor, dining at the hinge, living facing a
  full-width glazed opening to the terrace and garden.
- **Light, material, palette.** Natural light is the primary material; enlarged rear glazing
  turns the east elevation into a light wall. Surfaces stay tactile and restrained —
  large-format porcelain or microcement, white and greige walls, oak joinery, glass at every
  threshold. The palette retires the pinks and blues for paper-white, greige, oak, and a
  single forest-green drawn from the site.

## Spatial proposal

The lot runs ~11.5 m wide × ~23 m deep, street to creek. The footprint and structure are
kept; the plan is re-read in three bands:

- **Service / entry** — kept at the street edge (carport, access, stair foot, powder room).
- **Open social core** — the partition-free volume through the middle and rear.
- **Private** — bedrooms upstairs, with cleaned ceilings, refinished floors, reworked bathrooms.

The existing covered terrace becomes the indoor–outdoor bridge: porcelain run flush inside to
out, the bamboo ceiling retained, sliding glass that opens fully to the protected garden.

## Materiality

| Material | Use |
|---|---|
| Paper white | Walls · ceilings |
| Greige porcelain | Ground-floor floor |
| Oak / clay | Joinery · island |
| Forest green | Accent · garden tie-in |
| Bamboo (caña) | Existing terrace ceiling (retained) |
| Black frame | Glazing · fixtures |

Practical notes: favor porcelain over solid wood in wet and social zones, seal any retained
timber, specify mold-resistant systems, and keep cross-ventilation — the tropical climate
rewards durable, low-maintenance choices.

---

## Key interventions

Ordered by impact (1–3 deliver most of the transformation):

1. **Open the ground-floor social core** — remove non-structural partitions.
2. **Enlarge the rear glazing** — full-width opening to terrace and garden.
3. **Redesign the kitchen as an island** — the working anchor of the social core.
4. **Modernize all finishes** — porcelain/microcement, warm wood, white-and-greige palette.
5. **Reskin both bathrooms** — walk-in glass showers, floating wood vanities, matte-black fixtures.
6. **Lift the upstairs ceilings & refine the stair** — flatten dropped panels; visual verticality.

> **Structural caution.** In a two-story house, the walls bracketing the central stair and
> supporting the upper floor may be load-bearing. The open core and any double-height gesture
> depend on a structural engineer confirming load paths. That verification — not the design
> ambition — sets the true scope and budget.

---

## Conclusion

A renovation with a favorable risk profile: the heavy lifting is spatial and cosmetic, and
the protected green frontage is a durable advantage that money cannot otherwise buy. The
single most valuable design decision is orientation — turning the whole social sequence toward
the green, private edge.

**Recommended next steps:** architectural validation → structural review → finish & lighting
specification → phased budgeting (cost the high-leverage items 1–3 first).
